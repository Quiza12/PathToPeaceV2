Nervous System Regulation

Shaking

Shaking is an activity we can do at any time of the day to release built up stress in the body. When it comes to trauma and stress, the body keeps the score, and we can take care of it releasing stress that can develop into something worse.

Grounding

There's physical grounding (contact with the Earth which linked to improvements to energy, sleep, anxiety, chronic pain). Connecting to Earth in a physical way, skin to earth, in touch with the world around you. Mental grounding, I like to think of as the practice of slowing down and engaging with the present moment. Ways to ground can be through meditation, breathing, art, silence, journalling and affirmations. Especially acknowledging what's around you that you may not always notice, take the time to notice them and be grateful for all the little things that make the Earth habitable for us!

Tapping

EFT Tapping (Emotional Freedom Technique) consists of tapping on acupuncture points in the body to stimulate signals to the brain and calm and relax the us, especially in moments of distress and built up tension. You can even mix in some affirmations to really activate the energy you are transporting.

Humming

Humming is an incredible technique which can stimulate all sorts of benefical changes in the mind and body. Humming activates the vagus nerve, which sends signals to the brain that we are safe. It can also boost energy levels, helps induce sleep, and release oxytocin (the 'love' hormone).

Sleep

A primary determinant of our energy and stress levels on a day to day basis is sleep! Sleep duration, quality, hygiene (prioritising the bed for sleep and sex). Start taking small steps to improve your sleep and you see the world with less fear, alert and worry. Some simple tips for improvin sleep quality is shutting off electronics 1-2 hours before bed, having some tea, reading a book, breathing, humming etc.

Human Connection

Human connection is an absolute biological imperative. We adapted the freeze response from reptiles, the fight or flight response from mammals, and established our social engagement system where we are at our most present, joyful and safe. The nervous system strives to protect yes, but it also strives to connect.

Brain Stimulation

The Brain is ours to take care of! It needs to be challenged in order for the maitenance and replenishment of cells. Silent time can release a protein in the brain which helps maintain nuerons. Other examples of Brain Stimulation activities include: Sudoku, Crosswords, Think piece articles and Vdeos, Online games, Drawing, Chess, Jigsaw puzzles, etc. These are all activities which train the brain and keep it active. Healthy brain = Healthy Life!
